📐 What StructuralWind Does

Core Capabilities

  • Site-specific wind speed (Vsit,β) — calculated for all 8 cardinal directions using regional wind speeds, terrain, shielding, and topographic multipliers
  • Design wind pressure (qz) — with full AS/NZS 1170.2 methodology including importance levels and directional effects
  • External pressure coefficients (Cp,e) — for walls and roofs across all standard building shapes: flat, gable, hip, and monoslope
  • Internal pressure coefficients (Cp,i) — automatically calculated based on opening ratios
  • Net face pressures — combining external and internal pressures for each building surface
  • Interactive map — satellite/street view with building footprint overlay, wind direction arrows, and TC zone visualisation
  • 3D building model — real-time pressure heatmaps, dimension lines, and interactive face selection
  • 8-direction multiplier tables — edit Md, Mz,cat, Ms, and Mt per direction with Vsit,β wind rose
  • Professional PDF reports — export calculation summaries with all parameters, clause references, and 3D screenshots

📚 Standards Compliance

All calculations follow AS/NZS 1170.2:2021 — Structural design actions, Part 2: Wind actions. Specific clause references are provided throughout the application so results can be independently verified.

Key clauses implemented:

  • Clause 2.2 — Regional wind speed VR
  • Clause 2.4.1 — Site wind speed Vsit,β = VR Md Mz,cat Ms Mt
  • Clause 2.4.1 — Design wind pressure qz = ½ρair V²sit,β
  • Table 4.1(A) — Terrain/height multiplier Mz,cat
  • Table 5.2 — External wall pressure coefficients
  • Table 5.3 — External roof pressure coefficients
  • Clause 5.3 — Internal pressure coefficients Cp,i
  • Clause 2.5.3 — Net pressure p = qz (Cp,e − Cp,i) Ka Kc,e
